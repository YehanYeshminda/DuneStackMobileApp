Privacy Policy — DuneStack Places

Last updated: 7 July 2026

DuneStack Places ("the app") is a personal, on-device app for saving places you care about along with their location and your photos. This policy explains what the app does — and does not do — with your information.

The short version

The app does not collect, transmit, or sell your data. There are no accounts, no servers operated by us, no analytics, and no advertising. Everything you save stays on your device.

Information the app handles

  • Location. When you choose to save a place, the app reads your device's current GPS coordinates and stores them locally on your device so the place appears on your map and list. Location is only accessed at the moment you save a place.
  • Camera and photos. If you attach a photo to a place, the app uses your camera and stores the resulting image file locally on your device.
  • Place details. Titles, notes, and collections you create are stored locally on your device.

Where your data is stored

All of the above is stored only in the app's local database and file storage on your device. It is never uploaded to us or to any third party. Uninstalling the app removes this data.

When information leaves your device

Your data only leaves your device when you explicitly choose an action that sends it:

  • Open in Maps. Tapping to view a saved place in an external maps app (such as Google Maps or Apple Maps) hands that place's coordinates to that app, which is governed by its own privacy policy.
  • Share or export. If you use the share or export feature, you choose the destination (for example a messaging app or file location), and your data is sent only there.

The app also checks your device's network connection status to adjust what it shows. This check happens locally and sends no data.

Permissions

  • Location — to record where a saved place is.
  • Camera — to take photos you attach to a place.

You can revoke these permissions at any time in your device settings.

Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from them. Because no data is collected at all, no personal information about children is gathered.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this page with a revised "last updated" date.
